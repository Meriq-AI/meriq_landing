Meriq AI is building the AI-native pre-clearance workspace for customs brokers, freight forwarders, and trade teams.

Global trade does not break only at the filing system. It breaks before filing — inside scattered emails, incomplete shipment documents, unclear product descriptions, invoice and packing list mismatches, and endless follow-ups between importers, suppliers, forwarders, and brokers.

We are starting by helping teams turn messy shipment documents into broker-ready cases before filing.

Long term, our vision is to build the context layer for customs brokerage: a system that captures, organizes, and distills how experienced brokers handle exceptions — and eventually becomes an AI-native customs brokerage for global trade.

## 1. The problem we're solving

Before a shipment can be filed, trade teams spend hours doing manual coordination work that is repetitive, fragmented, and high-risk.

### Fragmented shipment data

Commercial invoices, packing lists, bills of lading, product specs, certificates, supplier emails, and customer replies are scattered across inboxes, PDFs, Excel files, portals, and messaging apps. No one has a clean source of truth for each shipment.

### Manual document review

Operators manually compare invoices, packing lists, and bills of lading to check product names, quantities, weights, values, consignee details, and missing fields. This work is tedious, but mistakes can create delays, extra warehouse costs, compliance issues, or disputes.

### Endless follow-ups

A single missing COO, MSDS, product use description, FDA-related detail, or unclear product name can trigger long email threads. Brokers, forwarders, importers, suppliers, and customers are often CC'd across many messages, and a case can sit for days or weeks before it is ready for review.

### Hidden broker knowledge

The most valuable knowledge inside a customs brokerage is not just how to file forms. It is how experienced brokers handle messy exceptions: what questions to ask, which documents are missing, when a product needs human review, and how to move a case forward safely.

### Outdated workflow

Most teams still rely on email, spreadsheets, local files, and tribal knowledge. Existing TMS or filing systems are systems of record, but they often do not solve the messy pre-filing work that happens before a case is ready.

## 2. Our solution: the pre-filing context layer

Meriq AI helps trade teams create a single source of truth for each shipment before filing. We connect and understand shipment documents, emails, and broker requests, then turn them into structured, reviewable cases.

### Document Intelligence

Meriq reads commercial invoices, packing lists, bills of lading, and supporting documents, then extracts shipment details into a structured case.

### Document Consistency Checks

Meriq compares key fields across documents — product descriptions, quantities, weights, values, parties, ports, and references — and flags mismatches that need review.

### Missing Information Detection

Meriq identifies missing documents, incomplete product details, vague descriptions, and fields that may require human confirmation before broker review.

### Follow-up Automation

Meriq generates clear follow-up requests for customers, suppliers, importers, or internal teams, and helps track what is still pending.

### Broker-Ready Case Handoff

Instead of forwarding a messy email thread, teams can hand brokers a clean case summary with uploaded documents, extracted data, missing items, resolved issues, and traceable evidence.

## 3. Our starting wedge

We are not starting by replacing customs brokers or filing systems. We are starting with the workflow before filing:

1. Create a shipment case
2. Upload invoice, packing list, bill of lading, and supporting documents
3. Extract shipment information
4. Check document mismatches
5. Detect missing information
6. Generate follow-up requests
7. Prepare a broker-ready case for human review

This is the highest-friction part of the workflow and the best place to earn trust.

## 4. The future we're building

The future of customs work is not more inboxes, spreadsheets, and manual chasing.

We believe every shipment should have a living context layer: a structured memory of what the shipment is, what documents support it, what is missing, what has been resolved, what needs expert review, and how similar exceptions were handled before.

In the near term, Meriq helps brokers and forwarders review documents faster and reduce back-and-forth.

Our goal is not to remove human expertise from global trade. Our goal is to capture and amplify it.

## 5. Our core principles

### Human judgment stays in control

Customs work involves regulation, liability, and trust. Meriq helps prepare, organize, and recommend, but licensed professionals and operators remain in control of final decisions.

### Context beats chat

The future is not a generic chatbot answering trade questions. The future is a system that understands each shipment, each customer, each document, and each exception in context.

### Traceability builds trust

Every extracted field, issue, and recommendation should be traceable back to the source document or operational history.

### Automate the follow-up, not the responsibility

Meriq automates repetitive coordination: finding missing data, drafting requests, tracking status, and preparing handoffs. Humans review outcomes and handle judgment-heavy exceptions.

### Start narrow, compound context

We start with pre-filing document readiness because it is painful, repetitive, and close to revenue. Over time, every reviewed case improves the context layer.
