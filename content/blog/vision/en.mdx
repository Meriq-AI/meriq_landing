Meriq helps customs brokers, freight forwarders, and trade teams turn scattered emails, shipment documents, product details, and regulatory questions into clear, traceable, reviewable shipment cases — before filing.

The real mess usually doesn't happen inside the filing system. It happens before filing.

Before a shipment enters formal declaration, teams typically have to work through supplier emails, the commercial invoice, packing list, B/L, product specs, photos, origin information, MSDS, HS / CCC classification questions, and permit or agency review issues. This information is usually scattered across different email threads, PDFs, Excel files, LINE, WhatsApp, and local folders.

Meriq's starting point is simple: teams shouldn't have to review every shipment from scratch.

You hand a shipment case to Meriq, and it reads the documents and communication history — extracting the key fields, cross-checking the documents for inconsistencies, flagging missing information, raising HS / CCC and permit-risk questions, and drafting the next questions to ask suppliers, customers, or the authorities.

Every reminder and suggestion links back to the source document or email, so brokers can review quickly instead of re-reading an entire thread.

We're not here to replace the broker's professional judgment, and we're not here to replace the official filing system.

What Meriq does is the most time-consuming, most fragmented, and most easily stuck preparation work before filing: organizing information, finding gaps, comparing for anomalies, preparing follow-ups, and preserving source evidence — so the experts can reach a decision faster.

In the near term, Meriq helps teams cut manual organizing and back-and-forth, moving more cases into broker review faster.

In the long term, Meriq becomes the context layer for customs brokers and trade teams: continuously accumulating each shipment's documents, questions, decisions, follow-ups, and exception-handling experience — systematizing senior staff know-how so teams can handle more, and more complex, shipment cases with less manual effort.
